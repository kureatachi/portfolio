My Messy Drawer
UIUX | Storytelling Interactive Media | 2025

Project Overview

Beginning as a children's storybook, My Messy Drawer explores the tension between the public self and the private self through the metaphor of a clean desk with a messy drawer hidden beneath it.

Concept & Inspiration

This project grew from the realization that identity is rarely as tidy as we present it. The metaphor of a neat desk with a messy drawer captures the tension between our controlled, outward-facing self and the hidden layers of uncertainty, vulnerability, and unfiltered thoughts we carry. Drawing inspiration from children's books, emotional regulation research, and personal narrative, My Messy Drawer examines how we organize, conceal, and reveal the parts of ourselves that don't fit neatly into everyday presentation.

Design Process

The design process began with extensive research into interactive storytelling, emotional design, and user experience patterns that create meaningful connections. I explored how physical metaphors translate to digital interfaces, studying the psychology of discovery and the power of hidden content.

Early iterations focused on creating an authentic "messy" aesthetic—one that feels organic and lived-in rather than artificially chaotic. The challenge was balancing visual disorder with usability, ensuring that the messiness enhances rather than hinders the user experience.

Typography, color, and interaction patterns were carefully considered to evoke a sense of intimacy and discovery. Each element was designed to feel like it has a history, like it belongs in this digital drawer that has been accumulating memories over time.

Interaction Design

The interaction model centers around exploration and discovery. Users navigate through the drawer by clicking, dragging, and hovering—each interaction revealing new layers of content. The origami letters can be "unfolded" to reveal their messages, creating moments of surprise and emotional connection.

I wanted the interactions to feel tactile and responsive, as if users are physically manipulating objects in a real drawer. This required careful attention to animation timing, feedback, and the choreography of how elements move and respond to user input.

The experience is intentionally non-linear, allowing users to discover content at their own pace and in their own order. This reflects the nature of memory itself—fragmented, associative, and deeply personal.

Technical Approach

The project leverages modern web technologies to create smooth, responsive interactions while maintaining the organic, handcrafted aesthetic. CSS animations and JavaScript handle the complex choreography of elements, while careful optimization ensures the experience feels fluid across different devices.

One of the key technical challenges was creating a sense of depth and layering within the drawer interface. This required careful z-index management and strategic use of shadows and opacity to create visual hierarchy and the illusion of objects stacked within a three-dimensional space.

Reflection

This project represents a departure from more traditional portfolio work—it's less about solving a specific problem and more about exploring a feeling, a concept, a way of being. Working on something so personal has been both challenging and liberating.

It has taught me about the power of vulnerability in design, about how digital spaces can hold emotional weight, and about the importance of creating experiences that allow for quiet moments of reflection. The "messiness" isn't just aesthetic—it's a deliberate choice to embrace imperfection and authenticity in digital design.

As I continue to develop this project, I find myself returning to it, adding new letters, new feelings, new layers. In many ways, it has become its own messy drawer—a space where I can explore ideas and emotions that don't fit neatly into other projects.
